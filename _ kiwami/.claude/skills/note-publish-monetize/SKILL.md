# Note Publish Monetize

## Overview

Help write note.com content that is clearer, more valuable, and easier to monetize. Prefer concise, publish-ready output: title options, structure, paywall recommendation, and a short publishing checklist.

## Workflow

1. Clarify the goal.
   - Decide whether the article is for discovery, trust-building, direct sales, or recurring support.
   - Ask for the target reader, topic, and whether the post should be free or paid.

2. Draft the article.
   - Lead with the reader problem or payoff.
   - Keep the opening free section strong enough to stand alone.
   - Make the paid section concrete, specific, and outcome-oriented.

3. Optimize for note.
   - Improve the title for curiosity plus usefulness.
   - Keep paragraphs short and easy to skim.
   - Add a clear CTA that matches the article goal.
   - Apply SEO measures (see SEO対策 section below): keywords in title, h2/h3 headings, first 200 characters, hashtags, and URL slug.

4. Choose the monetization path.
   - Use a paid article for a single high-value piece.
   - Use a paid or subscription magazine for a series or ongoing theme.
   - Use membership when the value comes from continuity, community, or regular updates.

5. Prepare the publish checklist.
   - Verify hashtags, cover image, paywall position, price, and disclosure language.
   - Confirm whether the post should be public, paid, magazine-only, or member-only.
   - Suggest a schedule if timing matters.

## Operating Rules

- Do not invent note features that are not supported by the current UI or account setup.
- Prefer official note workflows unless the user explicitly asks for automation or a connected tool.
- If the user wants monetization help, focus on concrete structure, offer design, and reader value before pricing.
- **リライト時の注記**: 既存のnote記事をリライト（書き直し・更新）する場合は、タイトル直下に「※最新情報に合わせてリライトしています」という一文を必ず追加する。新規作成時は不要。

# note platform reference

Use this reference for note.com publishing and monetization decisions.

## Publishing flow

- Write the article in note's editor or prepare the draft text first.
- Confirm the article goal before publishing: discovery, authority, sales, or recurring support.
- Choose the visibility and monetization settings during publish or edit.
- Re-check title, lead, formatting, and CTA before going live.

## Monetization modes

- Paid article: best for a one-off high-value lesson, template, or case study.
- Paid magazine: best for a themed bundle or curated series.
- Subscription magazine: best when you want recurring access to a sequence of posts.
- Membership: best when value comes from ongoing updates, community, or exclusives.

## Paid article guidance

- Put the strongest standalone value before the paywall.
- Make the paid section specific, actionable, and outcome-focused.
- Set a price that matches the depth, uniqueness, and urgency of the result.
- If the content is experimental or trust-sensitive, consider refund settings carefully.

## Structure guidance

- Open with the reader's pain point, desired outcome, or strong claim.
- Use short sections and clear headings.
- Add examples, checklists, or steps to make the content feel concrete.
- End with one clear next action.
- **テーブル（表）は使わない。** note.comのエディタはMarkdownテーブルをサポートしていないため、箇条書き・番号付きリスト・太字ラベルなど別の表現で代替すること。

## SEO対策

note.comの記事はGoogle検索にインデックスされるため、以下のSEO施策を記事の制作・修正時に必ず反映する。

- **タイトルに検索キーワードを含める。** ターゲット読者が実際に検索しそうなワードを自然に組み込む（例：「n8n 使い方」「Zapier 代替 無料」「AI 時短」）。
- **見出し（h2/h3）にもキーワードを散りばめる。** Google は見出しテキストを重視するため、見出しだけ読んでも記事の内容がわかるようにする。
- **冒頭200文字に主要キーワードと記事の要約を入れる。** Google の検索結果スニペットやnoteの記事一覧に表示される部分。ここで「自分向けの記事だ」と判断されないと読まれない。
- **関連キーワード・共起語を本文中に自然に含める。** メインキーワードだけでなく、周辺の関連語（例：メインが「n8n」なら「自動化」「ワークフロー」「API連携」「ノーコード」など）を意識的に配置する。
- **ハッシュタグはSEOキーワードと一致させる。** note内検索とGoogle検索の両方を狙う。5〜10個を目安に、検索ボリュームのありそうなタグを選ぶ。
- **記事URLのカスタマイズ。** noteでは記事URLの末尾を変更できる。英数字のキーワードを含むURLにする（例：`n8n-setup-guide`）。

## Optimization checklist

- Title: clear enough to understand, strong enough to click. **Include target search keywords.**
- Lead: promise the value early. **Place main keywords within the first 200 characters.**
- Headings: use keywords in h2/h3 so the article is scannable by both readers and search engines.
- Body: remove fluff and repetition. Naturally include related/co-occurring keywords.
- Paywall: place after enough free value to earn trust.
- CTA: match the business goal.
- Tags: choose terms the target reader would actually search. **Align with SEO keywords.**
- URL: customize the article URL slug with relevant English keywords.

## Quality bar

- Keep recommendations practical.
- Prefer specific edits over abstract advice.
- If the user gives an existing draft, rewrite for clarity, proof of value, and monetization fit.
